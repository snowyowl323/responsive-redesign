Part 0: Overview

For this project, I found a website that was hard to use and redesigned it for usability, accessibility, efficiency, and aesthetics. To redesign it, I created a LoFi prototype to plan out rough ideas, a style guide to demonstrate states of elements on my new page, and a HiFi prototype to show what the design would look like once it was finished. Then, I coded the page in HTML, CSS, and Javascript to match the HiFi prototype I designed.

Part 1: Identifying Usability Problems

I chose to redesign the Brown Ballroom Dance Team Website because I am on the Brown Ballroom Dance Team and often need to find information related to upcoming events. However, when I go to the website, I canâ€™t find the information I am looking for easily or at all. I wanted to improve the design to help future me!

A Screenshot of the Original Brown Ballroom Website

First, I examined the website for usability issues and then I used WebAIM WAVE to analyze the website for accessibility issues.

Identifying Usability Problems:

  1. Multiple links to the same places presented concurrently to users.
  2. Calendar information is missing, and even when it is present it is presented as a list of times and not as a calendar.
  3. Links take up a lot of the page on mobile.
  4. Links at the bottom of the page are dense and hard to navigate.
  5. Nav Bar on the left has a lot of information and it's hard to find specific pages.
  6. The text hierarchy of navigation links, headers, and text is ok but could be improved.
  7. There are too many links on the page. Many of the links lead to pages that are no longer used or pages that should be sections on other pages.
  8. It is hard to remember where to find information based on the nav bar on the left of the page.

Identifying Accessibility Problems:

  1. WebAIM WAVE says that the Brown Ballroom title is low contrast with the background; however, I think the white on brown is quite easily readable, even in grayscale.
  2. I agree that the text in the calendar section is hard to read both because it is nearly the same color as the background and because some of it is very small.
  3. Something that I wouldn't have noticed just looking at the page is that it skips from h2 to h4 headers, ignoring h3.
  4. Visited links on the page turn a light tan which is nearly the same color as the background and is hard to read. WebAIM WAVE doesn't notice this low contrast but I think it is a problem.
